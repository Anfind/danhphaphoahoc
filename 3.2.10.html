NAMING ALDEHYDES


NAME PRONUNCIATION AUDIO PRONUNCIATION
aldehyde /ˈæl.dəˌhɑɪd/

Click here to look up the name, audio pronunciation, condensed formula, Infrared spectroscopy (IR) and Mass spectrometry (MS) of aldehydes.


- Aldehydes are organic compounds containing a functional group −CH=O attached to carbon or hydrogen. The functional group itself (without the "R" side chain) can be referred to as an aldehyde but can also be classified as a formyl group.
- General molecular formula of saturated, monofunctional, open-chain aldehydes: CnH2nO (n ≥ 1) or CmH2m+1CHO (m ≥ 0).

1. Common name


- Some aldehydes are simply called by common names (from the common name of acids), which are related to their origin.
  FIRST WAY: acid name (without “acid”) + “ ” + aldehyde
